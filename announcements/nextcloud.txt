# Update: openDAW Now Talks to Your Own Nextcloud!

You can save and open your projects straight from a Nextcloud you control, right in the browser.
Samples and soundfonts are stored once and reused across projects, so nothing gets uploaded twice.

### Heads Up: It Is Self-Hosted
Nextcloud is open-source software you host yourself, so you (or any school) need to run a Nextcloud
server. There is not a quick sign-up service. Already have a server? Use the
[Nextcloud web install guide](https://nextcloud.com/install/).

### Your Files Stay Yours
Your files stay on your own server. openDAW connects to it directly from the browser. Nothing passes
through our servers.

### Good for Classrooms
A school can run its own Nextcloud and give each student an account, so every student signs in and
works in their own space.

### Get Started
Open the menu, then Nextcloud, then Browse or Upload. Read the
[admin setup guide](https://opendaw.studio/manuals/nextcloud).

openDAW now integrates with Nextcloud.

openDAW is a free, browser-based digital audio workstation. With the new Nextcloud integration,
schools and individuals can store and load their music projects on a Nextcloud server they run
themselves, directly from the browser.

Why it matters for education:
• Data ownership: projects and audio stay on the school's own Nextcloud. openDAW talks to it
  directly. Nothing is routed through or stored on our servers.
• Per-student accounts: each student works in their own space, isolated from the others.
• Efficient: samples and soundfonts are deduplicated, so shared content is stored once.

No vendor lock-in: projects live on the school's own Nextcloud, never on openDAW's servers, so the
school keeps full control of its data.

Free and open: https://opendaw.studio
Admin setup guide: https://opendaw.studio/manuals/nextcloud

Subject: openDAW for schools, now with your own Nextcloud

openDAW now connects to a school's own Nextcloud, so a music class can keep all its work on the
school's own server, with each student in their own space, straight from the browser.

Why this matters for schools
• Every student signs in with their own Nextcloud account and works in their own space, so one
  student cannot see or change another's projects.
• Every project, sample, and soundfont stays on the school's own Nextcloud. openDAW talks to it
  directly. Nothing is routed through or stored on openDAW's servers.
• The school stays in full control of its data, which makes it easy to satisfy data-protection
  requirements.
• Shared samples and soundfonts are stored once and reused across projects, saving space.

How it works for the class
The school runs its own Nextcloud (an open-source, self-hosted alternative to Dropbox or Google
Drive). The administrator creates an account per student once. From then on, students just open the
Nextcloud menu in openDAW and sign in to save and load their projects.

Getting started
You will need a Nextcloud where you are the administrator. Our step-by-step guide walks you through
the one-time setup and connecting from openDAW:
https://opendaw.studio/manuals/nextcloud
